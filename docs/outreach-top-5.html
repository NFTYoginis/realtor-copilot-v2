Sanity rules

  • Do not volume-pitch. These five are it. Adding more dilutes follow-up bandwidth and looks desperate.
  • Wait 5–7 business days before any follow-up. Editors are buried. A week of silence is not a no.
  • One follow-up max. If the second nudge gets nothing, move on. Submit a fresh pitch later when something material changes (cold-test data, new case study, traction numbers from HN).
  • Be honest about validation status. If asked "have agents tested this?", the truthful answer right now is "v1 has a documented simulated cold-test and real-agent validation is in progress with two practicing agents — cold-test data should be in within 1–2 weeks." Don't oversell.
  • Decline paid placement. Sponsored coverage will associate the project with a sales-y posture that contradicts the "discipline is the moat" framing.
  • Keep follow-up signal short. "Following up on the pitch I sent on [date] — quick yes/no easy?" beats a re-pitched paragraph.

What the AI cannot do for you

  • Cannot send the emails or post to HN — that requires your email account, your byline, your HN account.
  • Can only prepare the materials so the send takes minutes, not hours.
