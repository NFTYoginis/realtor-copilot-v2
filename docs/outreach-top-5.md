## A few sanity rules

- **Do not volume-pitch.** These five are it. Adding more dilutes follow-up bandwidth and looks desperate.
- **Wait 5–7 business days before any follow-up.** Editors are buried. A week of silence is not a no.
- **One follow-up max.** If the second nudge gets nothing, move on. Submit a fresh pitch later when something material changes (cold-test data, a new case study, traction numbers from HN).
- **Be honest about validation status.** If asked "have agents tested this?", the truthful answer right now is "v1 has a documented simulated cold-test and real-agent validation is in progress with two practicing agents — cold-test data should be in within 1–2 weeks." Don't oversell.
- **Decline paid placement.** Sponsored coverage will associate the project with a sales-y posture that contradicts the "discipline is the moat" framing. If anyone offers paid placement, decline politely.
- **Keep your follow-up signal short.** "Following up on the pitch I sent on [date] — quick yes/no easy?" beats a re-pitched paragraph.
