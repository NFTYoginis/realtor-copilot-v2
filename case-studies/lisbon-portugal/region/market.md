# Market — Lisbon, Portugal

Lisbon (Concelho de Lisboa) — the capital and largest urban market in Portugal. This pack covers central Lisbon plus the adjacent coastal premium market (Cascais/Estoril) when relevant.

## Working currency

EUR. Convert on request — common buyer currencies: USD, BRL, GBP, CHF.

## Working languages

Portuguese (primary). English (very widely spoken in Lisbon real-estate circles — most agents and lawyers operate in English fluently). French (relevant for French buyers — Lisbon has a meaningful French expat community). Brazilian Portuguese is mutually intelligible with European Portuguese but uses different vocabulary; agents serving Brazilian buyers should be aware.

## Inventory snapshot (early 2026)

- **Inventory:** moderate. Central Lisbon (Chiado, Príncipe Real, Estrela, Lapa, Avenida da Liberdade) is supply-constrained for premium apartments. Cascais coast premium villa supply is thin. Outer districts (Olivais, Marvila, parts of Alvalade) have more inventory but turnover faster.
- **Days on market:** Premium central apartments: 30–90 days for well-priced, 90–180+ for ambitious pricing. Cascais coast villas: 60–180 days typical.
- **Pricing trajectory:** Central Lisbon premium has plateaued from the 2018–2022 surge but holds value; high-quality renovated stock continues to clear; mid-tier inventory has softened modestly. Cascais coast remains firm. Outer districts experiencing more price discovery.

## Adjustment magnitudes for pricing memos

These are **starting baselines** — verify against current 90-day comps before relying on a specific number.

| Feature | Adjustment |
|---|---|
| Renovated kitchen + bathrooms (last 5 years) | +10–18% on a comparable unrenovated unit |
| Elevator in a building 4 floors+ | +€50–€150K depending on tier and buyer pool |
| Terrace / large balcony (>10 m²) | +€30–€100K depending on view + tier |
| Parking spot (central Lisbon) | +€25–€50K |
| Storage room (arrumos) | +€5–€15K |
| Mainland river view (Tejo) | +5–15%, varies sharply by quality |
| Pool (Cascais villas) | +€80–€200K depending on tier |
| Walk-up — no elevator, 3rd floor+ | -€20–€60K depending on tier; bigger hit for empty-nester / older-buyer pools |
| Ground floor (rés-do-chão) | -5–12% in central, neutral in Cascais |
| Listed/historic-protected facade or interior elements | Net positive on prestige + tax incentive eligibility, but constrains renovation scope; price net depends on buyer's renovation appetite |
| AL (Alojamento Local) license attached | Highly variable — significant premium where licenses are frozen for new issuance (most central Lisbon parishes), neutral where freely issuable |

## Foreign-buyer activity

| Buyer pool | Volume | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **North American** (US/Canada) | High | Lifestyle buyers (post-2020 remote-work flow), retirees on D7 visa, digital nomads on D8. Often English-monolingual. Frequent on premium central apartments (€800K–€2.5M) and Cascais coast. |
| **Brazilian** | High | Historic deep flow, often family-driven (kids' education, EU passport pathway). Investment + lifestyle. Operate in Brazilian Portuguese. |
| **French** | Moderate–high | Lifestyle (climate, cost-of-living relative to Paris); active in Estrela/Príncipe Real central + Cascais coast. Often prefer French-language documentation. |
| **British** | Moderate | Brexit + lifestyle; active in Cascais coast and central. Cash-buyer-heavy. |
| **Mainland Chinese** | Moderate, declining | Post-Golden-Visa (program closed for residential property in 2023), volume has fallen materially. Some residual activity. |
| **Other EU (German, Dutch, Scandinavian)** | Moderate | Lifestyle + retirement + digital nomad. Distributed across central + Cascais. |

## Visa-program context (matters for buyer framing — not legal advice)

The **Golden Visa** program closed for residential property purchases in October 2023. Investment paths now run through funds, R&D, and other non-residential-property categories.

Active visa pathways still relevant to incoming residential buyers:

- **D7 visa** — passive-income / retirement
- **D8 visa** — digital nomad (remote-work income from outside Portugal)
- **HQA / Tech / R&D pathways** — high-skill labor, research

These do not require property purchase but are common companions to one. **Always defer to immigration counsel for specifics.**

## Tax considerations (surface only — defer to a Portuguese accountant)

- **IMT (Imposto Municipal sobre Transmissões)** — transfer tax. Bracketed by purchase price; second-home and high-value brackets are higher.
- **IS (Imposto do Selo)** — stamp duty. Standard rate applies on the higher of declared price and tax value.
- **IMI (Imposto Municipal sobre Imóveis)** — annual property tax. Rate varies by municipality.
- **AIMI (Adicional ao IMI)** — additional property tax for high-value holdings.
- **Capital gains** — applies on disposal; NHR (non-habitual resident) status, where still applicable, has historically affected treatment.

For non-resident buyers and sellers, structuring matters significantly. Surface this early; never offer specific numbers.

## What this market is NOT

- Not a yield-investor market in the way central Tel Aviv or Bangkok periphery is. Gross residential yields in central Lisbon typically run 3–5%; Cascais coast often lower. Buyers in this market are dominantly lifestyle / capital appreciation / family / EU-pathway, not pure yield.
- Not a leasehold market. Standard freehold under Roman-law title — much simpler structurally than Khao Lak or central London.
- Not a high-volume agent market relative to U.S. metros. Transaction counts are modest; agent income depends on average-deal-size more than deal-frequency.
