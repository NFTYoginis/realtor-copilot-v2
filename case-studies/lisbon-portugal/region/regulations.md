# Regulations — Lisbon, Portugal

Surface only — never give binding legal or tax advice. Always defer to a Portuguese real-estate attorney and accountant for specifics. Tax rates, brackets, and program windows change frequently; verify before quoting any number.

## Transaction taxes

### IMT — Imposto Municipal sobre Transmissões (transfer tax)

- Levied on the buyer at purchase.
- Bracketed and graduated based on declared purchase price.
- **Distinguishes between primary residence (habitação própria permanente) and second-home / non-resident purchase.** Second-home and non-resident brackets are higher.
- Calculation runs against the higher of declared price and the *valor patrimonial tributário* (tax authority's assessed value).
- New-build or rehab-incentivized properties may have specific rules.

### IS — Imposto do Selo (stamp duty)

- Levied at a flat rate on the higher of declared price and tax value.
- Surfaces in the closing costs alongside IMT.

### Mortgage-related stamp duty

- Additional stamp duty applies on the mortgage instrument when financed.

## Annual property taxes

### IMI — Imposto Municipal sobre Imóveis

- Annual property tax. Municipality sets the rate within a national range.
- Lisbon municipality rate is one of the higher ones; verify current.

### AIMI — Adicional ao IMI

- Additional tax for property holdings above a threshold tax-value.
- Applies in tiers.
- Affects high-end buyers and portfolio holders.

## Disposal — capital gains

- Capital gains on disposal taxed differently for residents vs. non-residents.
- Residents historically see partial exclusion when reinvesting in primary residence (verify current rules).
- Non-resident treatment has shifted in recent years; structuring matters.
- NHR (non-habitual resident) status, where applicable, has historically affected treatment for some income types.

## Short-term rental — Alojamento Local (AL)

A specific regulatory layer relevant to any buyer with short-term-rental intent.

- AL requires registration. Issuance is **frozen in many central Lisbon parishes** (containment zones) — meaning new licenses cannot be issued in those areas, but existing licenses can transfer with the property. This makes AL-licensed properties materially more valuable in containment-zone parishes.
- The 2023 housing legislation (Mais Habitação) imposed restrictions and changed the regime; later legislation has revised parts of it. **Verify current status before any AL-driven valuation.**
- Buyer's intent matters: some buyers want AL income (license status decisive), others want a primary residence (license irrelevant), others want the option (price for the option but verify transferability).

## Visa programs (relevant context for international buyers — never give legal advice)

### Golden Visa (closed for residential property, 2023)

- The Golden Visa program **closed for residential property purchases in October 2023.**
- Investment paths now run through qualifying funds, R&D contributions, and other non-residential-property categories.
- Existing Golden Visas (granted pre-closure) continue to operate under their original terms.
- Buyers asking about Golden Visa as a residential-property pathway should be redirected to immigration counsel — the residential path is closed.

### D7 — passive-income / retirement visa

- Requires demonstrable passive income above a threshold (pensions, investments, rental income from outside Portugal).
- Common pathway for retirees and trust-fund-eligible buyers.
- Property purchase is not required but is a frequent companion.

### D8 — digital nomad visa

- For remote workers earning income from outside Portugal above a threshold.
- Two sub-tracks: temporary (up to 1 year) and residency (path to longer stay).
- Common pathway for North American and European remote-worker buyers.

### HQA, Tech, R&D pathways

- For high-skill labor, research, and innovation-track applicants.
- Less directly tied to property but appears in some buyer profiles.

**Always defer specifics to immigration counsel.** The above is context for buyer framing only.

## Title and registration

- **Caderneta Predial** — the tax registry document for the property (Finanças). Identifies the property and its tax-value classification.
- **Certidão de Teor / Certidão Permanente** — the title registry document (Conservatória do Registo Predial). Confirms ownership, encumbrances, mortgages.
- Both should be pulled before commitment. Discrepancies between Caderneta and Certidão are common and need resolution before close.

## Heritage and listing protections

Many central Lisbon buildings — especially Chiado, Baixa, Alfama, and Avenida da Liberdade — have full or partial heritage classifications (Imóvel de Interesse Público, Imóvel de Interesse Municipal, individual element classifications).

Implications for buyers:

- Renovation scope is constrained. Façade work, window types, original elements may require approval from cultural-heritage authority (DGPC or municipal counterpart).
- Approval timelines can be long.
- Tax incentives may apply to qualifying restoration work — verify with accountant.

## Energy certification

- All listings must have an energy certificate (Certificado Energético) at time of sale.
- Class A through F. Pre-listing certificate cost is modest. Surface for buyers — affects long-term cost expectations and is a common contract checkpoint.

## Common gotchas for international buyers

- **Source-of-funds documentation.** Required by Portuguese banks and (depending on transaction structure) the notary. Surface early; international wires can fail at the AML check if documentation is thin.
- **NIF (taxpayer number)** required to transact. Easy to obtain; foreigners typically use a Portuguese accountant or *representante fiscal* to set up.
- **Power of attorney** common for remote close.
- **Currency hedging.** EUR-denominated purchase from a USD/GBP/BRL income base; large swings between LOI and close are real risk for some buyers.
- **Bank account** opening can be slower than expected for some nationalities; start early.
