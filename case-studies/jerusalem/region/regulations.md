# Region — Regulations & Tax

## Purchase tax / transfer tax

- **Tax name:** Mas Rechisha (מס רכישה) — Israeli purchase tax
- **Brackets / rates for resident buyers (single home):** 0% on first ~₪1.97M (indexed annually). Progressive thereafter, top bracket reaches 10%. Additional brackets for second-home and investment-property purchases.
- **Brackets / rates for non-resident or foreign buyers:** Starts at 8% on the entire purchase price; higher brackets (up to 10%) on higher-value properties. No "first-home" exemption available for non-residents.
- **Notable exemptions:** Olim (new immigrants) — preferential rates within first 7 years of aliyah, subject to conditions. Family transfers — reduced rates with qualifying conditions.

## Capital gains tax on disposal

- **Tax name:** Mas Shevach (מס שבח)
- **Standard rules:** Resident sellers — primary-residence exemption available subject to conditions. Standard rate ~25% on net capital gain after exemptions. Holding period and renovation costs reduce taxable gain.
- **Foreign-seller / non-resident rules:** 25% rate typically applies; structuring matters. Inheritance and family-transfer rules can be complex for non-residents. Always verify treaty implications with Israeli accountant + home-country counsel.

## Property tax / annual fees

- **Local name:** Arnona (ארנונה)
- **How calculated:** By zone (Jerusalem has a tiered zoning scheme) × apartment area × residential rate
- **Who pays in a rental scenario:** Tenant typically pays.
- **Vaad Bayit (ועד בית):** Building committee fee — separate from Arnona, covers shared building maintenance. Usually paid by occupant.

## Ownership registration

- **Title types in this market:**
  - **Tabu (טאבו)** — full Land Registry registration. The standard, preferred form. Clean title with formal lien-and-encumbrance check available.
  - **Khevra Meshakenet (חברה משכנת)** — older form, common in pre-1990s buildings. Title held with a housing company; must verify standing before transfer.
- **What an agent should verify before transfer:**
  - Tabu extract (or Khevra Meshakenet status confirmation)
  - Tofes 4 (טופס 4) — occupancy permit
  - Building permits — verify no unpermitted additions
  - Vaad Bayit — outstanding obligations or special assessments
  - Liens, mortgages, registered claims
- **Common gotchas:**
  - Khevra Meshakenet conversions in transition
  - Unpermitted balcony enclosures in older buildings
  - Inheritance situations with multiple heirs

## Foreign-buyer rules

- **Restrictions on foreign ownership:** Generally permitted. Border-area and nationally-sensitive properties restricted. East Jerusalem ownership has specific legal complexities — always verify with counsel.
- **Additional taxes or surcharges:** Mas Rechisha non-resident bracket. No annual foreign-owner surcharge.
- **Documentation requirements:** Passport + visa or residency status; source-of-funds (AML); Israeli bank account often required; Israeli accountant or attorney typically engaged before purchase.
- **Power-of-attorney for remote close:** Common; must be notarized and apostilled per Hague Convention if signed abroad.
