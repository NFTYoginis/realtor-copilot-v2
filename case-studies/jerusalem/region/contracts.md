# Region — Contracts & Standard Terms

## Residential lease — typical terms

- **Term length:** 12 months standard. 5-year leases common in Israel for apartments. Short-term (less than 6 months) for vacation rentals subject to municipal regulation.
- **Renewal norms:** Often automatic with prior notice. Termination notice typically 30 days for short leases, 60–90 days for longer terms.
- **Deposit / security:** Typically 2–3 months' rent + bank guarantee or post-dated checks.
- **Indexation / rent escalation:** CPI-linked indexation common in 2+ year leases.
- **Common contingencies:** Tenant covers minor repairs; landlord covers major systems. Pets typically negotiated.
- **Who pays what:**
  - **Arnona:** Tenant typically pays.
  - **Vaad Bayit:** Tenant typically pays.
  - **Utilities:** Tenant pays.
  - **Major repairs and structural:** Landlord.

## Sale agreement — typical terms

- **Deposit structure:** ~7% earnest money at signing; balance at closing or at registered milestone (e.g., post-Tofes 4 verification).
- **Common contingencies:**
  - Financing (if buyer is using a mortgage)
  - Tabu extract showing clean title
  - Tofes 4 (occupancy permit) verified
  - Building permits in order
  - No outstanding Vaad Bayit obligations
- **Closing timeline:** 60–90 days from signing. Foreign-buyer transactions often longer (90–120 days) due to remote-close logistics.
- **Documents the buyer must provide:** Israeli ID or passport + visa (foreign buyers); source-of-funds documentation; mortgage pre-approval if applicable.
- **Documents the seller must provide:** Recent Tabu extract (or Khevra Meshakenet status); Tofes 4; recent Arnona statement; Vaad Bayit clearance; building permits / blueprints; Mas Shevach calculation.

## Common gotchas

- **Tabu vs. Khevra Meshakenet status:** Older buildings (pre-1990) sometimes still in Khevra Meshakenet. Verify at the start, not at closing.
- **Tofes 4 issues:** Missing or expired occupancy permits can block transfer. Common in older buildings or those with unpermitted additions.
- **Unpermitted construction:** Closed balconies, illegal extensions, rooftop additions. Either disclosed and resolved, or potential block to Tabu transfer.
- **Vaad Bayit obligations:** Special assessments for major repairs (elevator, roof, Tama 38 retrofitting) can create outstanding obligations transferred at sale.
- **Tama 38 status:** Some buildings are mid-renovation under Tama 38 / Tama 38/2 (earthquake retrofit). Buyers must understand current status and timeline.
