# Region — Market Overview

## Market identifier

- **Market name:** Jerusalem, Israel
- **Last updated:** 2026

## Buyer segments

- **Local end-users:** Israeli families across observance levels — secular, traditional, Modern Orthodox, Haredi. Pricing varies dramatically by neighborhood and observance fit. Value lifestyle, school proximity, and community.
- **Returning diaspora / olim:** Significant segment. Anglo (U.S., U.K., Canada, South Africa) — typically Modern Orthodox or traditional, family-driven, school-focused. French — wave from 2014–2020 has decelerated but ongoing, concentrated in specific neighborhoods. Russian-speaking — established community, varying observance levels.
- **International investors:** Active. Yield investors target multi-unit buildings in central neighborhoods. Capital-appreciation investors target prime real estate (Old City, German Colony, Mamilla). Some second-home buyers (especially Anglo) overlap with diaspora segment.
- **Domestic investors:** Israeli investors increasingly look outside Tel Aviv for value. Often interested in multi-unit, short-term rental, or pre-renovation flips.
- **Ultra-Orthodox:** Distinct sub-market with its own neighborhoods (Mea Shearim, Har Nof, Geulah, Sanhedria) and dynamics.

## Seasonality

- **Spring (post-Pesach through early summer):** Peak. Aliyah arrivals, school-cycle buyers acting before September, foreign-buyer visits.
- **Summer (Tammuz–Av):** Slow. Heat, schools out, vacation. Religious mourning period reduces social activity.
- **Pre-Rosh Hashanah:** Modest activity.
- **Sukkot week:** Effectively dead.
- **Pre-Pesach:** Pickup as families plan for summer move.

The Jewish calendar matters more than the civil calendar. Pesach and Sukkot windows are unsuitable for most active showings.

## Current dynamics

- Tight supply in prime central neighborhoods (German Colony, Old Katamon, Baka, Talbiya, Rehavia). Renovated units transact quickly.
- Stable demand from Anglo aliyah, particularly in Modern Orthodox neighborhoods.
- French wave decelerated post-2020 but ongoing demand persists in specific clusters.
- Old City and Mamilla continue to attract international investor interest.
- Periphery neighborhoods (Pisgat Ze'ev, Gilo, Har Homa) have weaker price appreciation than central.
- Currency: NIS-denominated rents in a stable urban submarket. USD investors should consider hedging or pricing assets as USD-equivalent.

## Foreign buyer activity

- **Active segment?** Yes — significant.
- **Origins:** U.S., U.K., Canada, France, South Africa, Russia, Argentina. Smaller Asian segment (Chinese, Singaporean) — investor-driven, concentrated in specific assets.
- **Predominant motivations:** Lifestyle/legacy (most common — diaspora identity, future relocation, family legacy). Capital appreciation (concentrated buyers). Yield (smaller segment, focused on multi-unit).
- **Estimated share of transactions:** 15–25% of central Jerusalem residential transactions involve a foreign or non-resident buyer (varies by neighborhood and quarter).
- **Working languages:** Hebrew + English routine. French and Russian as needed in specific clusters.
