# Region — Neighborhoods

The major Jerusalem neighborhoods this agent works in. Per `frameworks/buyer-fit.md`, these inform location-fit scoring.

---

## Old Katamon (Katamon HaYeshana)

- **Character:** Dense Modern Orthodox and traditional Jewish community. Mix of original 1920s–50s stone buildings and mid-rise additions. Walkable, residential, with a strong neighborhood feel.
- **Typical buyer:** Modern Orthodox families, often with school-age kids; returning Anglo or French diaspora; multi-generational Jerusalem families.
- **Community / observance context:** Heavily Modern Orthodox. ~5+ minyanim within 10-minute walk. Mixed Sephardic/Ashkenazic congregations. Strong English-speaking community.
- **School access:** Multiple Modern Orthodox elementary and high schools nearby; well-rated public elementary.
- **Price band:** ₪3M–8M for typical 3–4BR apartments. Renovated penthouses can exceed ₪10M.
- **Transit / access:** Central; walking distance to commercial Emek Refaim. Limited street parking.
- **Friction points:** Many older buildings without elevators. Limited parking. Pre-Pesach and pre-Sukkot construction creates noise.
- **Foreign-buyer relevance:** Yes — high concentration of Anglo and French diaspora buyers and second-home owners. Critical neighborhood for this segment.

## Baka

- **Character:** Quiet, residential, predominantly low-rise. Mix of original Mandate-era homes and mid-rise apartments. Family-oriented.
- **Typical buyer:** Modern Orthodox and traditional families; Anglo aliyah; downsizing local families looking for quiet.
- **Community / observance context:** Strong Modern Orthodox infrastructure. Multiple congregations. English-speaking community present but smaller than Old Katamon.
- **School access:** Public elementary schools well-regarded. Religious day-school options nearby.
- **Price band:** ₪2.5M–6M for typical 3–4BR apartments. Garden apartments and smaller standalone houses can exceed ₪7M.
- **Transit / access:** Bus-served; near Derech Beit Lechem commercial. Walking distance to German Colony.
- **Friction points:** Older stock often lacks elevator. Some streets have heavy through-traffic.
- **Foreign-buyer relevance:** Yes — meaningful Anglo and family-relocation segment.

## German Colony (HaMoshava HaGermanit)

- **Character:** Premium central neighborhood. Mix of restored Templar-era homes, prewar apartment buildings, and luxury new construction. Known for café and restaurant strip on Emek Refaim.
- **Typical buyer:** Affluent local Israelis, Anglo professionals, French diaspora, foreign second-home buyers.
- **Community / observance context:** Mixed religious/secular. Strong English-speaking community. Multiple congregations across observance levels.
- **School access:** Several private and Anglo-friendly schools nearby. Public schools also strong.
- **Price band:** ₪5M–15M+. Restored Templar houses can reach ₪20M+.
- **Transit / access:** Walkable. Bus access. Tourist-corridor traffic during peak season.
- **Friction points:** Limited parking. Tourist traffic. Higher commercial activity than other residential neighborhoods.
- **Foreign-buyer relevance:** Yes — top destination for foreign buyers (Anglo, French, diaspora). Important for capital-appreciation thesis.

## Talbiya

- **Character:** Luxury, government-adjacent. Larger original houses converted to multi-unit; some preserved as single-family. Quieter than German Colony.
- **Typical buyer:** Affluent locals, foreign second-home buyers (particularly French and Anglo), diplomats and government officials.
- **Community / observance context:** Mixed religious/secular. Less of a community-walking-distance feel than Baka or Old Katamon.
- **School access:** Less family-school-driven; older residents and second-home buyers dominate.
- **Price band:** ₪7M–25M+ for apartments and converted houses.
- **Transit / access:** Central; near Knesset and government quarter.
- **Friction points:** Less family-walking-community character. Some buyers find it quieter than expected.
- **Foreign-buyer relevance:** Yes — key destination for high-end foreign and second-home buyers.

## Rehavia

- **Character:** Central, prewar, secular-leaning. Tree-lined streets, mid-rise residential. Near Knesset.
- **Typical buyer:** Affluent secular and traditional locals; older population; some foreign-buyer interest.
- **Community / observance context:** Less religiously dense than Old Katamon or Baka. Mixed observance.
- **School access:** Public schools well-regarded.
- **Price band:** ₪4M–10M for typical 3–4BR.
- **Transit / access:** Central; near light rail. Limited parking.
- **Friction points:** Older stock; limited parking. Less family-walking-community character.
- **Foreign-buyer relevance:** Moderate — some interest, less concentrated than German Colony or Talbiya.

## Old City — Jewish Quarter (Rova HaYehudi)

- **Character:** Historic, dense, walkable within the quarter. Stone construction. Religious-observant population dominant.
- **Typical buyer:** Strongly observant Modern Orthodox or traditional families; foreign investors interested in Old City exposure; legacy/heritage buyers.
- **Community / observance context:** Heavily religious-observant; multiple yeshivot and synagogues; Kotel walking distance.
- **School access:** Religious schools within walking distance; family-school-walking-community is essential here.
- **Price band:** ₪5M–20M+ for typical apartments. Heritage premium significant.
- **Transit / access:** Walking only within the quarter. Vehicle access via specific gates and routes.
- **Friction points:** Security considerations during periods of regional tension. Vehicle access restrictions. Renovation requires specific approvals (heritage zone).
- **Foreign-buyer relevance:** Yes — Old City exposure is a specific buyer segment (heritage, legacy, religious significance). Different framing than central neighborhoods.

## Pisgat Ze'ev (Northern Jerusalem)

- **Character:** Suburban, newer (1980s onward), large-family neighborhood. Mid-rise residential. Affordable relative to central.
- **Typical buyer:** Local Israeli families, particularly larger families; first-time buyers priced out of central.
- **Community / observance context:** Mixed observance; predominantly traditional/Modern Orthodox.
- **School access:** Multiple religious and public schools.
- **Price band:** ₪1.8M–3.5M for typical 3–5BR apartments.
- **Transit / access:** Light rail connects to central Jerusalem. Bus service. Car-friendly.
- **Friction points:** Distance from central employment and commercial corridors.
- **Foreign-buyer relevance:** Limited — not a typical foreign-buyer destination.
