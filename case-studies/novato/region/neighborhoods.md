# Region — Neighborhoods

The major Novato neighborhoods this agent works in.

---

## Pleasant Valley

- **Character:** Established suburban; single-story and split-level homes from the 1960s–80s. Family-oriented, low-density.
- **Typical buyer:** Marin families; Bay Area outflow tech families; long-time residents trading houses within the neighborhood.
- **Community / observance context:** N/A (no specific religious or community concentration).
- **School access:** Pleasant Valley Elementary well-rated; San Marin High School district. Strong school perception drives demand.
- **Price band:** $1.2M–1.5M for typical 4BR SFH; renovated and view properties higher.
- **Transit / access:** Quiet residential streets. 10-minute drive to Highway 101. Limited public transit.
- **Friction points:** Traffic on Atherton Avenue during commute hours. Some streets have older sewer and water infrastructure.
- **Foreign-buyer relevance:** No.

---

## Hamilton (Hamilton Field)

- **Character:** Newer, planned community on the former Hamilton Air Force Base. Built primarily 1990s–2010s. Modern, low-maintenance.
- **Typical buyer:** Younger families, Bay Area tech outflow, first-time-in-Marin buyers.
- **School access:** Hamilton Elementary; Hamilton K–8 charter. Generally well-regarded.
- **Price band:** $1.0M–1.4M for typical 3–4BR. Townhomes and smaller SFH at lower end.
- **Transit / access:** Closer to Highway 101 than older Novato neighborhoods. SMART train station within reach.
- **Friction points:** Mello-Roos special tax adds to ongoing cost. Some sub-developments have HOAs with ongoing fees.
- **Foreign-buyer relevance:** No.

---

## Bel Marin Keys

- **Character:** Waterfront community on Marin's bayside. Boat-dock and lifestyle properties. Mid-century architecture, many with private docks.
- **Typical buyer:** Lifestyle buyers (boat owners, water-access seekers); empty-nesters; second-home buyers from Bay Area.
- **School access:** Same district as central Novato.
- **Price band:** $1.5M–2.5M+ for waterfront properties; non-waterfront homes lower.
- **Transit / access:** Removed from main commercial Novato; longer drive to Highway 101.
- **Friction points:** Waterfront maintenance (docks, sea walls). Wind. HOA covering common waterways and channels.
- **Foreign-buyer relevance:** Limited — some lifestyle buyers but not typical foreign-investor target.

---

## Marin Country Club / North Novato

- **Character:** Older established neighborhood adjacent to the Marin Country Club. Larger lots, mature landscaping, many homes from the 1970s–80s.
- **Typical buyer:** Established families, country-club members, longer-term Marin residents.
- **School access:** Pleasant Valley district.
- **Price band:** $1.4M–2.2M for typical 4BR; larger properties higher.
- **Transit / access:** Quiet residential streets. Country club proximity adds amenity for some buyers.
- **Friction points:** Larger lots mean more landscaping cost. Some streets steep / hillside.
- **Foreign-buyer relevance:** No.

---

## Old Town Novato (Downtown)

- **Character:** Walkable downtown with historic homes from early 20th century. Smaller lots. Mix of residential and commercial.
- **Typical buyer:** Walkability-seekers, downsizers, first-time buyers, lifestyle buyers wanting downtown access.
- **School access:** Standard Novato Unified district.
- **Price band:** $900K–1.3M for typical 2–3BR homes; rare 4BR can reach higher.
- **Transit / access:** Walkable to downtown shops, restaurants, SMART train station nearby (commute to San Rafael, San Francisco).
- **Friction points:** Smaller lots; some homes need significant updating; downtown noise on commercial streets.
- **Foreign-buyer relevance:** No.
