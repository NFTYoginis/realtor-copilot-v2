# Region — Contracts & Standard Terms

## Residential lease — typical terms

- **Term length:** 12-month standard. Month-to-month after initial term common.
- **Renewal norms:** 30-day notice typical for month-to-month termination.
- **Deposit / security:** California limits to 2x monthly rent (unfurnished). Refundable subject to itemized deductions.
- **Indexation / rent escalation:** Year-over-year increases regulated under California rent-control laws (AB 1482) — annual cap of CPI + 5%, max 10% in any 12-month period (subject to exceptions).
- **Common contingencies:** Standard CA lease form; pet policies negotiated.
- **Who pays what:**
  - **Property tax:** Owner.
  - **Utilities (electricity, water, gas, trash):** Typically tenant; owner sometimes pays trash and water.
  - **Major repairs:** Owner. Tenant covers minor (per CA Civil Code).

## Sale agreement — typical terms

- **Standard form:** California Association of Realtors (CAR) Residential Purchase Agreement.
- **Deposit structure:** Typically 3% earnest money to escrow; balance at close.
- **Common contingencies:**
  - Inspection (pest, general, sometimes pool/well/septic)
  - Loan / financing (if buyer is financing)
  - Appraisal contingency
  - Title contingency
- **Closing timeline:** 30–45 days from accepted offer; 21-day fast closes possible with cash and clean title.
- **Documents the buyer must provide:** Loan pre-approval (if financed); earnest money deposit; disclosure acknowledgments.
- **Documents the seller must provide:**
  - Transfer Disclosure Statement (TDS) — required CA disclosure
  - Natural Hazard Disclosure (NHD) — required CA disclosure (flood, fire, earthquake, environmental)
  - Lead-based paint disclosure (pre-1978 homes)
  - HOA documents (if applicable)
  - Well/septic certifications (if applicable)
  - Preliminary title report

## Common gotchas

- **Prop 13 reassessment:** Buyers often surprised that property tax basis resets to purchase price at close. Critical to surface in pricing discussions.
- **NHD / Wildfire zones:** Increasingly relevant in Marin. Some Novato properties in or near wildfire zones; buyer insurance may be expensive or limited.
- **Mello-Roos:** Newer Hamilton-area developments — adds annual cost; not always disclosed prominently.
- **Easements:** Utility and conservation easements on semi-rural Novato properties.
- **HOA budget health:** Hamilton-area sub-developments. Verify reserve studies and any pending special assessments.
- **Septic and well systems:** Indian Valley and other semi-rural Novato properties. Maintenance and replacement costs.
