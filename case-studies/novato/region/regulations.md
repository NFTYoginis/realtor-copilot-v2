# Region — Regulations & Tax

## Purchase tax / transfer tax

- **Tax name:** California real estate transfer tax (state) + city/county transfer tax
- **Brackets / rates for resident buyers:** State transfer tax ~$1.10 per $1,000 of sale price. Marin County typically does not add a county transfer tax beyond state. Novato city: verify whether local transfer tax applies.
- **Brackets / rates for non-resident or foreign buyers:** Same as resident; no foreign-buyer surcharge in CA. (FIRPTA withholding applies to foreign **sellers**, not buyers.)
- **Notable exemptions:** Inter-spousal transfers exempt. Gift transfers may apply.
- **Note on property tax basis (Prop 13):** Property tax assessed value resets at purchase to the new sale price. This can be a significant cost shock for buyers — surface in pricing memos.

## Capital gains tax on disposal

- **Tax name:** Federal capital gains + California state capital gains
- **Standard rules:** Federal: $250,000 single / $500,000 married primary-residence exemption. Holding-period thresholds (long-term vs. short-term). California: conforms to federal in many areas.
- **Foreign-seller / non-resident rules:**
  - **FIRPTA:** 15% federal withholding on gross sale price for foreign sellers (subject to reduction with proper certifications).
  - **California real estate withholding:** 3.33% of sale price (for non-resident sellers).

## Property tax / annual fees

- **Local name:** Property tax (Prop 13)
- **How calculated:** ~1% of assessed value + local additions (school bonds, special districts, Mello-Roos in some neighborhoods). Marin County typically 1.0–1.3% effective rate.
- **Who pays in a rental scenario:** Owner pays (typical California practice).
- **Mello-Roos:** Special tax assessment districts; relevant in newer subdivisions. Not present in older Novato neighborhoods like Pleasant Valley, but worth checking for Hamilton-area properties.

## Ownership registration

- **Title types in this market:**
  - Fee simple (standard)
  - Condominium (less common in Novato 4BR SFH segment)
  - Trust ownership (common for estate-planning purposes; verify trust structure)
- **What an agent should verify before transfer:**
  - Recent title report (preliminary title before contract, full title insurance at close)
  - Easements (utility, conservation, right-of-way)
  - Well/septic disclosures (some Novato semi-rural properties)
  - HOA disclosures (if applicable — common in newer Hamilton-area subdivisions)
- **Common gotchas:**
  - Easement issues — particularly in Indian Valley and semi-rural Novato
  - Well/septic — required disclosures and ongoing maintenance considerations
  - HOA budget and reserve health — relevant for Hamilton planned communities

## Foreign-buyer rules

- **Restrictions on foreign ownership:** Generally permitted; no California restriction. Some federal scrutiny for cash purchases above thresholds in certain LLC structures.
- **Additional taxes or surcharges:** None at state level (no foreign-buyer surcharge).
- **Documentation requirements:** AML/KYC at lender level for financed purchases; cash purchases face FinCEN GTO scrutiny in some metros (San Francisco, Los Angeles) but not currently triggered for Marin.
- **Power-of-attorney for remote close:** Permitted; less common than in international markets.
