## Buyer segments

For each major segment in your market: who they are, what they want, what they pay. Skip segments that don't apply.

- **Local end-users:** Modest segment. Thai families and Phang Nga / Takua Pa locals working in tourism and services. Concentrated in inland and back-road locations away from beachfront pricing. Typically buying smaller Thai-style houses and townhouses; not the core market for foreign-marketed listings.
- **Returning diaspora / olim / expats:** Long-stay Western expats, predominantly Northern European retirees (German, Swedish, Norwegian, Danish, British, Swiss). Often 50+, on retirement visas (O-A / O-X / LTR). Buying for lifestyle — climate, slower pace, lower cost of living than home. Mid-market budgets.
- **International investors:** The dominant high-value segment. European holiday-home buyers (Germany and Scandinavia first, UK and Switzerland behind), Russian buyers (volatile post-2022 — track currency and visa policy), growing Chinese and Korean interest, scattered U.S. and Australian buyers. Motivation mix: lifestyle holiday home + rental yield through hotel-managed condo programs. Most transactions in the foreign-marketed segment involve this group.
- **Domestic investors:** Smaller. Bangkok-based investors buying resort-managed condos for yield + occasional personal use. Less significant than foreign investor flow.
- **First-time buyers:** Not really a Western-style category here. Most foreign buyers are second-home or retirement buyers, not first-time.
- **Other significant segments:** Hospitality investors buying multi-key small resorts, dive-shop owners, restaurateurs — commercial-residential blends.

## Seasonality

When does your market move? Slow months? Religious or cultural calendars that affect timing?

- **High season (Nov–Apr):** Cool/dry months. Tourist arrivals and viewing activity peak Dec–Feb. Most foreign-buyer transactions originate from buyers physically in Khao Lak during this window.
- **Low season (May–Oct):** Southwest monsoon. Heavy rain, rough seas, many small resorts and beach businesses close or reduce. Viewings drop sharply. Use this window for relisting prep, photography refresh (overcast skies are honest but not aspirational — schedule shoots for shoulder months), and seller-side groundwork.
- **Sensitive date:** December 26 is the anniversary of the 2004 Indian Ocean tsunami. Avoid promotional pushes around that date locally; many residents and long-stay expats observe it.
- **Chinese New Year (late Jan / early Feb):** Spike in Chinese viewings; consider Mandarin marketing assets in advance.

## Buyer-disclosure conventions

What this market expects to be surfaced proactively in listings and showings — even when not legally required. The listing template references this section; populate it with disclosures that matter for the typical buyer pool here.

- **Tsunami / coastal-hazard context (post-2004).** Coastal Khao Lak property buyers — particularly the European retiree pool — commonly want to know elevation, distance from shore, and whether the property is inside/outside the official tsunami evacuation zone before a showing. Surface proactively in listings for any property within ~1 km of the beach. Avoid defensive language; state the facts and reference the local evacuation plan if the property is in a designated zone.
- **Building elevation / flood history (monsoon).** Inland and back-soi properties: surface elevation and any monsoon flood history. Bang Niang / Nang Thong inland sub-sois have variable drainage.
- **Construction year vs. building code.** Buildings predating 2005 may not reflect post-tsunami construction standards. Surface year built; note if structural retrofit was done.
- **Lease registration date and remaining years (leasehold villas).** Always state in the listing — never market a "30-year lease" without the actual remaining term.
- **Foreign-quota status (condos).** If marketing to foreign buyers, confirm the condo's 49% foreign quota has headroom at the time of listing. State it.
- **Short-term rental restrictions.** Many condo juristic persons restrict daily rentals. If the property is in a building with such restrictions, surface upfront — buyers expecting Airbnb yield will be disappointed late if not.
