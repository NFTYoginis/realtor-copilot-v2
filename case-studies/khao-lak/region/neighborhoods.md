## Nang Thong (นางทอง)

- **Character:** The commercial heart of Khao Lak. Walking-distance to restaurants, bars, dive shops, ATMs, supermarkets. Mid-density by Khao Lak standards (still mostly low-rise 2–4 storeys). Mix of older guesthouse-converted condos and newer boutique projects.
- **Typical buyer:** First-time Khao Lak buyer who wants amenities at the door. Solo retirees, couples without a car, short-stay holiday-home users.
- **Community / observance context:** Most foreigner-facing area. English-speaking restaurants, expat-run businesses. Buddhist temples nearby; no specific religious-observance constraints relevant to housing.
- **School access:** International school options are limited in Khao Lak proper — most school-age expat families commute to Phuket (~1 hour) or homeschool. Local Thai schools available.
- **Price band:** Condos [VERIFY] ฿3M–฿8M; townhouses / shophouses [VERIFY] ฿4M–฿10M.
- **Transit / access:** Highway 4 (Phetkasem) runs through. Walkable for daily life. Phuket airport ~1 hour drive south.
- **Friction points:** Beach access requires crossing Highway 4 in some sub-blocks. Nightlife noise on the main strip. Limited true beachfront stock — most beachfront here is hotel-occupied.
- **Foreign-buyer relevance:** High. The default "first Khao Lak property" zone for European retirees and holiday-home buyers.

## Bang Niang (บางเนียง)

- **Character:** Just north of Nang Thong. Quieter than Nang Thong but still walkable to the Bang Niang night market and beachfront restaurants. Mix of small condo projects, pool villas in side-sois, and older Thai houses.
- **Typical buyer:** Second-time Khao Lak buyer trading up from Nang Thong; mid-budget pool-villa buyer who wants quiet but not isolation.
- **Community / observance context:** Strong long-stay European expat community (German and Scandinavian particularly). Bang Niang night market is a community fixture (currently operates several nights per week — verify current schedule).
- **School access:** Same as Nang Thong — limited local international school options.
- **Price band:** Condos [VERIFY] ฿3.5M–฿9M; pool villas [VERIFY] ฿8M–฿20M+ depending on lot, pool size, beach proximity.
- **Transit / access:** On Highway 4. Beach access via several public side-sois.
- **Friction points:** Some inland sub-sois have limited drainage in heavy monsoon. Confirm flood history per lot.
- **Foreign-buyer relevance:** Very high. Strong resale liquidity into European buyer pool.

## Khuk Khak (คึกคัก)

- **Character:** Northern Khao Lak, broader area covering both beachfront resort strip and inland village. More resort-dominated on the beach side; quieter Thai-village character inland.
- **Typical buyer:** Beachfront condo buyer (high-end resort-managed projects on the beach). Inland: buyers seeking more land for less money, often willing to drive for daily errands.
- **Community / observance context:** Less concentrated expat community than Nang Thong / Bang Niang. More Thai local life.
- **School access:** Same regional limitation.
- **Price band:** Beachfront condos [VERIFY] ฿8M–฿25M+; inland villas [VERIFY] ฿6M–฿15M.
- **Transit / access:** On Highway 4. Spread out — a car is realistically required for inland Khuk Khak.
- **Friction points:** Inland properties often far from amenities. Confirm road quality and water supply per lot.
- **Foreign-buyer relevance:** High at the beachfront resort tier; mid for inland villa stock.

## Bang Sak (บางสัก)

- **Character:** North of Khuk Khak. Further out, quieter, longer stretches of less-developed beach. Newer pool-villa developments have expanded here in the 2020s.
- **Typical buyer:** Buyers who want more privacy, larger lots, and don't mind a 10–15 min drive to Khao Lak's restaurants. Often German / Scandinavian retirees.
- **Community / observance context:** Sparse expat presence; quieter Thai community.
- **School access:** Same regional limitation.
- **Price band:** Pool villas [VERIFY] ฿7M–฿18M+; land + build [VERIFY] varies widely.
- **Transit / access:** Highway 4. Driving distance to Khao Lak amenities.
- **Friction points:** Some new developments are inland from the beach — verify actual beach distance vs. marketing claims. Construction quality varies sharply between developers; due diligence on builder track record matters more here.
- **Foreign-buyer relevance:** High for the new-build pool-villa segment.

## La On (ลาออน) / South Khao Lak

- **Character:** South of Nang Thong, towards the Khao Lak-Lam Ru National Park border. Mix of resorts, smaller hotels, and pockets of villa development. Quieter than the main Nang Thong / Bang Niang strip.
- **Typical buyer:** Couples and retirees seeking the south-Khao-Lak balance of "near amenities but not in the strip."
- **Community / observance context:** Light expat presence, more resort-dominated.
- **School access:** Same regional limitation.
- **Price band:** [VERIFY]
- **Transit / access:** Highway 4. ~10 min to Phuket airport drive than other sub-areas.
- **Friction points:** Limited grocery / errand options in the immediate area.
- **Foreign-buyer relevance:** Mid.

## Pakarang / Pakweep Cape (ปาการัง)

- **Character:** Cape jutting into the Andaman north of Bang Sak. Premium resort and villa real estate, with several high-end branded developments. Some of the most expensive land in greater Khao Lak.
- **Typical buyer:** Top-tier holiday-home and beachfront-villa buyers. International executives, branded-residence buyers, ultra-high-net-worth retirees.
- **Community / observance context:** Resort-bubble character; less integrated with local Thai community.
- **School access:** Same regional limitation.
- **Price band:** Branded villas [VERIFY] ฿25M–฿100M+.
- **Transit / access:** ~15–20 min north of Khao Lak center. A car is required.
- **Friction points:** Distance from town amenities. Premium pricing has limited resale liquidity outside the same buyer tier.
- **Foreign-buyer relevance:** Very high — primarily an international-buyer market.
