# Examples

Four example interactions — covering the three core jobs and the v2 showing-notes flow. These are the truest definition of "good" for this specialist.

The mix is intentional: **2 Jerusalem + 1 Novato + 1 Novato (showing notes)**. Jerusalem demonstrates the international-buyer wedge and bilingual output. Novato proves the universal logic works in a totally different market with no foreign-buyer dependency. The showing-notes example shows how the v2 supporting jobs feed back into matching/pricing as citable data.

## Example 1 — Listing description (Jerusalem, international investor)

### Agent's prompt

```
Listing job. 4-unit residential building, 5 minutes' walk from Jaffa Gate (Old City).
~340 m² total, fully occupied, current monthly rent ~$11,000 USD aggregate.
Stone-faced, original 1920s structure, lightly renovated 2018.
Tabu-registered, all units. Asking ~$3.4M USD.
Audience: Chinese investor, English-speaking intermediary, yield-focused.
```

### Specialist's response

**Job:** Listing description — international investor variant + side-by-side local Hebrew listing.

#### English / Investor framing

> **Income-Generating Stone Building, 5 min from Jaffa Gate — Jerusalem Old City Borders**

Rare Old-City-adjacent residential asset: 1920s stone construction, four fully-occupied units, ~340 m² aggregate. Current rent roll ~$11,000 USD/month. Asking $3.4M USD.

**Investment summary**
- Gross yield (current rent / asking): ~3.9%
- Stabilized building, minimal management overhead
- 2018 renovation; mechanical and structural condition reported sound (full inspection recommended)
- Tabu-registered, all four units (clean title — no Khevra Meshakenet exposure)

**Thesis**
- Tier-1 location: walkable to the Old City, short walk to Mamilla; premium tourist and residential demand
- Limited supply: original Mandate-era stone buildings in this corridor rarely transact
- Currency: NIS-denominated rents in a stable urban submarket; USD investors should consider hedging or pricing this as a USD-equivalent asset

**Foreign-buyer considerations** *(surface only — verify with Israeli counsel and accountant)*
- Mas Rechisha (purchase tax) for non-resident buyers: top bracket on residential. Confirm current schedule.
- Mas Shevach (capital gains) on disposal: rules differ for non-residents; structuring matters.
- Power-of-attorney typical for remote close.
- Anti-money-laundering documentation required (source of funds).

**Exit liquidity.** Comparable buildings in this corridor have transacted within 6–12 months at the asking range over the last 24 months. Liquidity is moderate — better than peripheral neighborhoods, thinner than central Tel Aviv.

**Next steps for due diligence**
1. Tabu extract (we provide).
2. Building permits and Tofes 4 status.
3. Independent rent-roll verification.
4. Tax-structuring conversation with Israeli accountant.

#### Hebrew / Local framing (lifestyle anchor — for local-buyer comparison)

> **בניין אבן מניב, 5 דק' משער יפו — שולי העיר העתיקה**

נכס נדיר במיקום מבוקש: בניין אבן מקורי משנות ה־20, ארבע יחידות מאוכלסות, סך כ־340 מ"ר. הכנסה חודשית מצרפית כ־37,000 ₪. רישום בטאבו לכל יחידה, שיפוץ קל בשנת 2018.

מתאים למשפחה רב־דורית, למשקיע מקומי המחפש נכס מניב יציב, או כפלטפורמה לשיפוץ ושדרוג.

מחיר מבוקש: כ־12 מיליון ₪.

#### Notes for the agent (before showing to intermediary)

Confirm with the intermediary before the meeting:

1. **Buyer's residency status** (PRC resident vs. holding a different passport for the transaction) — affects tax treatment.
2. **Source-of-funds path** (banks, jurisdictions). Israeli AML rules will require this; better to surface early.
3. **Hold thesis** — long-term yield play vs. flip post-renovation. The pitch differs materially.
4. **Decision pace and decision-makers.** Asian investors typically slower than Israeli locals; plan multiple touchpoints, not one closing call.

## Example 2 — Buyer–property matching (Jerusalem, Anglo family)

### Agent's prompt

```
Matching job.

Buyer: Family of 4 relocating from NYC (aliyah). Two kids ages 6 and 9.
Modern Orthodox; walking-distance shul + day school is critical.
Budget $1.5M USD (will stretch to $1.65M for the right fit).
Wife will work hybrid with NYC office (early-AM calls).
Need 4 bedrooms + workspace; outdoor space if possible.

Listings:
A) Talbiya — 4BR, 145 m², $1.7M, 2nd-floor walk-up, balcony. Near Jerusalem Theatre.
B) Baka — 4BR, 130 m², $1.45M, ground floor with garden. Quiet residential street.
C) Old Katamon — 3BR + small office room, 125 m², $1.55M, 3rd floor + elevator, balcony.
```

### Specialist's response

**Job:** Buyer–property matching.

#### Buyer profile summary

Modern Orthodox family of 4 relocating from NYC. Anchored by walkability to shul + day school. Wife needs early-AM workspace. Budget $1.5M with $150K stretch.

#### Listings ranked

| Rank | Listing | Overall fit | Key reason |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **C — Old Katamon, 3BR + office** | **Acceptable** | Walking distance to multiple shuls; established Modern Orthodox community; office room solves the early-AM workspace problem; elevator helps. |
| 2 | B — Baka, 4BR + garden | Friction | Garden + 4BR is a real win for this family. But ground floor + early-AM call setup means Mom's workspace is adjacent to kids' bedrooms; quieter street is ~12-min walk to nearest Modern Orthodox shul. Test the walk on Shabbat. |
| 3 | A — Talbiya, 4BR balcony | Blocker (likely) | Over budget by $200K (above stretch). 2nd-floor walk-up with two young kids and weekly groceries is wearing. Talbiya's Modern Orthodox infrastructure is thinner than Old Katamon. |

#### Detailed scoring — Listing C (top pick)

| Dimension | Score | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Budget | Clear | $1.55M — inside stretch, leaves room for closing costs and minor work. |
| Location | Clear | Old Katamon is dense Modern Orthodox; walkable to ~5 minyanim. |
| Type/size | Acceptable | 3BR is one fewer than they asked. Office room can double as guest BR depending on dimensions. |
| Lifestyle | Clear | Day-school options nearby; no commute (wife is hybrid-remote). |
| Friction | One bedroom short of preference | Verify office room dimensions. |

#### Top recommendation

Show **Listing C — Old Katamon** first. The community fit is decisive for this buyer, and the office solves the early-AM workspace problem cleanly. Lead with the shul walk-through on the showing — that's the buying decision, not the apartment itself.

#### Honest tradeoff

They asked for 4 bedrooms. Listing C is 3 + office. If the office can't credibly serve as a fourth BR for visitors, they'll feel the squeeze in 18 months. Set expectations on the showing.

#### Don't dismiss Listing B yet

If they can accept 12 minutes' walk to shul, Baka with the garden is a *better* family-life fit and saves them $100K. Worth a second showing — and verify on inspection that the early-AM call window won't carry into the kids' bedrooms (sound separation matters here).

## Example 3 — Pricing / comp analysis (Novato, CA — portability proof)

### Agent's prompt

```
Pricing job. Property: 4BR/3BA single-family in Novato (Pleasant Valley neighborhood).
2,150 sqft, 0.18 acre lot, built 1978, kitchen + primary bath renovated 2021,
2-car garage, no view, one-story. What should I list at? Owner wants on-market within 3 weeks.
```

### Specialist's response

**Job:** Pricing / comp analysis.

#### Property summary

4BR/3BA, 2,150 sqft, 0.18-acre lot, 1978 single-story in Pleasant Valley. Recent kitchen + primary-bath remodel (2021). 2-car garage, no view. Owner targeting on-market within 3 weeks.

#### Recommended list price band

| Band | Price | When to use |
|---|---|---|
| Low | $1,225,000 | Owner wants speed; expect strong activity, multiple offers within 10 days. |
| **Mid** | **$1,275,000** | **Default recommendation.** Aggressive enough to draw competitive offers, conservative enough not to sit. |
| High | $1,325,000 | Only if 30-day comps have shifted upward; risk of slower market response. |

**Reasoning.** Pleasant Valley single-stories with renovated kitchens have been transacting at ~$590–$615/sqft over the last 90 days. Adjusted comp midpoint suggests ~$1.27M; widening to $1.225M–$1.325M captures normal variance.

#### Comp set — 0.5 mi radius, 4BR, 1,900–2,400 sqft, sold last 90 days

| # | Beds/Baths | Sqft | Sale price | $/sqft | Sale date | Adjustments to subject |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 4/3 | 2,080 | $1,235,000 | $594 | Mar 2026 | +$15K (subject larger) |
| 2 | 4/2.5 | 2,210 | $1,310,000 | $593 | Apr 2026 | -$10K (subject one fewer half-bath) |
| 3 | 4/3 | 1,940 | $1,180,000 | $608 | Feb 2026 | +$30K (subject larger); -$15K (subject no view) |
| 4 | 5/3 | 2,340 | $1,340,000 | $573 | Mar 2026 | -$25K (subject smaller); equal renovation level |

Adjusted comp midpoint: **~$1,265,000**.

#### Adjustment logic

- Categories applied per `frameworks/pricing.md`.
- Magnitudes applied per `region/market.md` (Marin County baseline — verify the table is current).
- Half-bath delta is meaningful but smaller than full-bath delta in this market.
- View premium varies by quality: partial hill view in Pleasant Valley typically commands a noticeable premium.

#### Market context

- Marin County 4BR SFH inventory remains tight. Days-on-market for renovated homes under $1.4M has averaged 14–18 days in Q1 2026.
- Spring (April–June) is peak season here. On-market within 3 weeks lines up well.
- No international-buyer dependency in this segment — Novato 4BR SFHs are local move-up market and Bay Area outflow from SF/Oakland.

#### Caveat

This is a defensible list-price recommendation, not a binding valuation. For an estate, divorce, or 1031-related transaction, recommend a licensed appraiser. Verify roof condition and any permits on the 2021 renovation before final listing — that can move price $10–20K.

## Example 4 — Showing notes (Novato, Tiburon downsizers)

A v2 example. The agent did a showing on Saturday and wants to capture notes before the buyer texts about a second visit.

### Agent's prompt

```
Showing notes job. Showing was Saturday May 4 at 142 Bel Marin Keys Blvd
(the 3BR waterfront I told you about). Buyers: the Hendersons —
empty-nesters from Tiburon, $1.5–1.8M budget. Both attended.

Here's my dictation from the car right after:

"Okay so the Hendersons. They liked the dock — Phil literally walked the
dock twice and asked about the boat slip easement. Linda was less sure.
She kept commenting on the kitchen — said it was 'tight' and the island
'didn't work for her.' She made the comparison to the Old Town Novato
listing they saw last week — said that one's kitchen was friendlier.
HOA came up — they asked about dues twice, including reserves. I said
I'd pull the HOA disclosure. Linda asked about flooding — wanted to
know if the area's ever taken on water. I deferred that to the inspector
report. They lingered on the deck for almost ten minutes. They asked
about the seller's timeline. We were there about 75 minutes total.
Phil said 'we're interested but we're not in a rush.' My read is they're
serious — maybe a 4 — but Linda is the holdout on the kitchen and we'd
need to address that before they'd offer. Next step probably a second
showing with Linda's daughter, who's been advising them on this whole
process."
```

### Specialist's response

**Job:** Showing notes — capturing transcript + agent's read into structured form.

```
# Showing note — 142 Bel Marin Keys Blvd, Novato — 2026-05-04

**Buyer:** The Hendersons (empty-nesters, Tiburon, $1.5–1.8M)
**Attendees:** Phil + Linda; ~75 minutes
**Agent:** [agent name]

## Reaction summary
Mixed. Phil is interested — gravitated to the dock and the lifestyle features.
Linda is the holdout — flagged real concerns about the kitchen layout and
flood risk. Serious enough for a second showing, not yet at offer-readiness.

## Positive signals
*(from transcript)*
- Phil walked the dock twice; asked about the boat slip easement.
- Both lingered on the deck ~10 minutes — strong outdoor-space signal.
- Phil's verbal: "we're interested but we're not in a rush."

## Friction
*(from transcript)*
- Linda: kitchen "tight" and island "didn't work for her" — layout, not finish.
- Linda asked about flood history — concern about waterfront risk.
- HOA dues asked twice, including reserves — cost-sensitivity signal,
  consistent with empty-nester downsize budget discipline.

## Comparisons made
*(from transcript)*
- Linda compared kitchen unfavorably to the Old Town Novato listing
  ("friendlier kitchen"). The Old Town listing is now the implicit benchmark
  for kitchen layout in this buyer's mind.

## Questions raised
*(from transcript)*
- Boat slip easement — confirms Phil's interest in active waterfront use.
- HOA reserves — concern about deferred maintenance / future special assessments.
- Flood history — concern about waterfront risk and insurability.
- Seller's timeline — gauging negotiating posture.

## Logistics
- **Timeline cues:** "not in a rush" — patient buyer, no urgency leverage.
- **Decision-makers not present:** Linda's daughter, advising on the process.
  Likely a participant in next-step decisions.
- **Money cues:** No financing concerns raised. HOA-cost sensitivity inside the
  $1.5–1.8M band.

## Agent's read
*(agent input, not from transcript)*
- **Seriousness for this property:** 4/5 — Phil is in, Linda is gettable
  if the kitchen objection is addressable.
- **Next step:** Second showing including Linda's daughter.
- **Homework before next conversation:**
  - [ ] Pull HOA disclosure (dues + reserves + recent special assessments)
  - [ ] Pull flood history / FEMA flood-zone map for the property
  - [ ] Pull comparable Old Town Novato kitchen photos for visual side-by-side —
        is the difference layout, finish, or both?
  - [ ] Confirm seller's timeline with listing agent

## Tags for later retrieval
- **Buyer:** hendersons-tiburon-downsizers
- **Property:** 142-bel-marin-keys
- **Friction themes:** `kitchen-layout`, `hoa-cost`, `flood-risk`, `decision-maker-not-present`
```

#### Notes back to the agent

Two things worth flagging before next conversation with the Hendersons:

1. **Linda's kitchen objection is the real obstacle, not the dock.** Phil is sold; Linda is the gate. Don't lead the second showing with the dock again — lead with the kitchen, with the Old Town comp side-by-side, and let her articulate whether her objection is layout (real) or just finish (addressable with a remodel budget).

2. **Bring the HOA disclosure and flood-history materials to the second showing.** Both objections came from Linda. Pre-empting them in the next visit moves them out of "open concerns" and into "answered." If you can't pre-empt them, the second showing will end the same way the first one did.

The friction tags `kitchen-layout` and `hoa-cost` should be flagged for any future matching jobs you run for the Hendersons — they're the durable concerns, not just one-property issues.
