# Checklist — Buyer Intake

Structured intake for the matching job. The specialist uses this to build a buyer profile before scoring listings.

## When this is consulted

Per `rules.md` routing: every matching job, before scoring listings.

## The questions

### 1. Household composition
- Adults
- Kids — number and ages
- Pets
- Expected household changes in the next 2 years

### 2. Budget
- Range — low end and stretch
- Currency
- Financing status — cash / pre-approved / not yet approved
- Lender, if applicable

### 3. Geography
- Must-be neighborhoods or zones
- Nice-to-have neighborhoods
- Hard no-go neighborhoods or zones
- Distance constraints — to work, school, family, religious community

### 4. Property type and size
- Apartment vs. house vs. open
- Minimum bedrooms and bathrooms
- Outdoor space — required / nice / optional
- Parking — required count
- Floor (apartments) — preferred / acceptable / no-go

### 5. Lifestyle
- Commute — destination, mode, frequency
- Schools — which, importance
- Religious or community observance — relevance to neighborhood choice
- Language environment — preference for community in their first language
- Other quality-of-life anchors

### 6. Timeline and motivation
- When must they move
- Why now
- Flexibility on timeline
- What happens if they don't find something — alternative plan

### 7. End-user vs. investor
- End-user (will live in the property), OR
- Investor — yield-focused, capital-appreciation-focused, or hybrid
- For investors: yield target, hold horizon, exit thesis

### 8. Past search context
- What they ruled out from prior searches and why
- What they almost bought and why they didn't
- What they regret about a previous home (informs what they want now)

## Missing-input behavior

If any item above is missing, the specialist:

1. Asks the agent for the missing items, listed.
2. Does not produce a fit summary until they're answered or explicitly waived.
3. If waived, scores listings using only the available dimensions and notes which were missing.
